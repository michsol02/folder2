ABOUT YOUR BUSINESS

Responsible 100 is a broad church...

SHARED AIMS & VALUES

We’re supported by a wide range of civil society organisations and businesses of all sizes, sectors and starting points. We welcome all organisations that share our aims and are comfortable with some or all of the following ideals, values and attributes.

1

A New & Distinct Approach

Responsible 100 is not offering a new way of doing CSR, nor does it support the idea that by 'doing our bit' we can somehow achieve the necessary change. It is a root and branch examination of how business impacts people and planet. We explore the externalisation of costs, and the exploitation of vulnerable stakeholders and precious resources, in search of alternatives. We work collaboratively to determine how businesses can deliver the goods and services we all need while systematically reducing and ideally eradicating such impacts.

2

Radical Ambition 

We must work within the existing rules and norms. Significant shifts in policy and practice can only be achieved when reward and incentive are created, and risks are minimised and shared. While businesses can only do what is within their purview and powers, Responsible 100 aims to substantially increase both. Businesses have enormous untapped potential to make the world a better place. Responsible 100 seeks to uncover this potential and to put it to the best possible uses.

3

Inside Out

What happens inside organisations matters externally. Employee activism is stronger than ever. Poor internal practices are quickly exposed, while a healthy culture is co-created, vibrant and obvious to all stakeholders. Engagement, productivity and loyalty among employees are increased when the leadership is accountable and transparent, and where common purpose is established. The Responsible 100 process encourages and enables active and regular employee involvement and contribution. Identifying, shaping and adopting meaningful changes will likely depend upon it.

4

...and Outside In

Businesses with the confidence to invite scrutiny from NGOs and campaign groups tap into huge reserves of goodwill and support. "Critical friends" also exist within unions, trade and investor groups, government and academia. More often than not, they want to work with businesses to help them rise to the social and environmental challenges they face. By bringing outsiders in, businesses avail themselves of new ideas, perspectives, and ways of doing things.

5

Campaigning & Political

Reengineering our economic system will require political will and urgency. Only by speaking and acting up, together, can we create it and build the necessary energy. More than ever, people want to know what businesses stand for, what they believe in, what they’re aiming for, and how they contribute. According to the latest Edelman research 87% believe stakeholders are more important than shareholders to long-term company success. Through participants’ genuine commitment to determining the necessary changes, and to making them, they can be loud and proud, open, confident and campaigning. They can join and lift the debate, shift expectations, and redefine what is possible. The time is now.

It's for people who are passionate about making a difference. Not for those who want to talk through the theory of how to make a difference, but those keen to learn about what works in today's context to ready their organisations for the future. "
